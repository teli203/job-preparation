Interview

Preparation

1. One Week Before the Interview: Preparation is Key

Research the Company:

Demonstrates your genuine interest in the role and company. Do the research compile your findings and it will show in the interview.

Understand the Job Description:

Helps tailor your answers to align with what the employer is seeking, and what you are capable of bringing to the team.

Know Your Interviewers:

Builds rapport and allows you to anticipate their priorities. To do this requires you to research prior to the interview and to do active listening when the interviewer speaks.

Plan Your Outfit:

Dressing appropriately boosts confidence and shows professionalism. Reseach shows that an individual is more confident when dressed in their best attire. If you have to buy your best attire, try to think of it as an investment on your future self.

2. Three Days Before the Interview: Polish Your Responses

Practice Common Questions:

Prepares you to answer confidently and concisely.

Prepare for Role-Specific Questions:

Shows your expertise and preparedness.

Create Your Own Questions:

Demonstrates interest and engagement.

3. The Day Before the Interview: Final Touches

Revisit Key Notes:

Refreshes your memory on critical details.

Test Your Tech (for Virtual Interviews):

Ensures a smooth, professional experience.

Plan Your Route (for In-Person Interviews):

Avoids delays and stress on the interview day.

Prepare Materials:

Shows you're organized and prepared.

4. The Day of the Interview: Show Time

Arrive Early:

Shows punctuality and professionalism.

Mind Your Body Language:

Communicates confidence and enthusiasm.

Engage Actively:

Builds a connection with your interviewer.

End on a Strong Note:

Leaves a positive final impression.

5. After the Interview: Follow-Up

Reflect on Your Performance:

Helps you improve for future interviews.

Stay in Touch:

Keeps you top-of-mind with the employer.

How:

Politely follow up if you haven't heard back after the stated timeline

Send a Thank-You Note (Within 24 Hours):

Reinforces your interest and professionalism.
